Current Studies

Explore our ongoing research projects in vision rehabilitation, neural interfaces, and assistive technology development. Each study is designed to advance the field and improve outcomes for individuals with visual impairments.

Active Research Projects

Our current research portfolio encompasses cutting-edge studies in neural rehabilitation, assistive device development, and clinical validation of new therapeutic approaches. All studies are conducted following international ethical guidelines and GCP standards.

Advanced Neural Stimulation for Vision Restoration

Recruiting

This clinical trial investigates the efficacy of targeted neural stimulation protocols for restoring visual function in patients with retinal degenerative diseases. The study combines state-of-the-art neural interface technology with personalized stimulation patterns based on individual cortical mapping.

Study Type
Phase II Clinical Trial
Duration
24 months
Participants
40 patients
IRB Approval
2024-003-KR

Primary Objectives:

  • Evaluate safety and efficacy of neural stimulation protocols
  • Measure visual acuity improvements in treated patients
  • Assess long-term stability of neural interface implants
  • Develop personalized stimulation optimization algorithms

AI-Powered Adaptive Visual Prosthetics

In Progress

Development and testing of intelligent visual prosthetic devices that adapt to user behavior and environmental conditions in real-time. This study integrates machine learning algorithms with advanced sensor arrays to create more intuitive and effective assistive visual devices.

Study Type
Device Development & Testing
Duration
18 months
Participants
25 volunteers
Funding
NRF Grant 2024

Key Goals:

  • Develop adaptive AI algorithms for real-time visual processing
  • Test device performance in various environmental conditions
  • Evaluate user satisfaction and learning curves
  • Optimize battery life and device portability

VR-Based Rehabilitation Training Protocols

Data Analysis

Investigation of virtual reality-based training programs for enhancing residual vision and improving functional outcomes in low vision patients. The study examines the effectiveness of immersive VR environments in promoting neural plasticity and visual skill development.

Study Type
Intervention Study
Duration
12 months
Participants
60 patients
Completion
85% complete

Research Focus:

  • Design effective VR training environments for vision rehabilitation
  • Measure improvements in visual processing speed and accuracy
  • Assess patient engagement and training compliance
  • Develop standardized VR rehabilitation protocols

Biocompatible Neural Interface Materials

Laboratory Phase

Comprehensive testing of novel biocompatible materials for long-term neural interface applications. This study focuses on material properties, inflammatory responses, and long-term stability of neural electrode arrays for visual prosthetic applications.

Study Type
Materials Testing
Duration
30 months
Testing Phase
In vitro & In vivo
Collaboration
International Partners

Testing Parameters:

  • Biocompatibility assessment and inflammatory response
  • Electrical conductivity and signal quality over time
  • Mechanical stability and device longevity
  • Manufacturing scalability and cost optimization
