Pan-retinal Full Correction and Myopia Control

Research on optical intervention methods to suppress myopia progression by analyzing the relationship between peripheral retinal hyperplane position and ocular growth, with focus on controlling the FS' focal line position of off-axis rays.

Research Overview

This study systematically analyzes the relationship between peripheral retinal focal position and ocular growth, which is the core mechanism of myopia progression. Specifically, we aim to identify the influence of the FS' focal line position (among the two focal lines FS' and FT' created by astigmatism from off-axis rays reaching the retina) on ocular growth, and develop effective myopia suppression methods.

This research introduces the concept of complete correction considering the refractive state of the entire retina, moving beyond conventional central refraction correction, and proposes optical intervention methods that can fundamentally block myopia progression.

Key Research Mechanisms

1. Peripheral Retinal Hyperplane Position and Ocular Growth

  • FS' (Sagittal Focal Line): Acts as a primary signal promoting ocular growth. When this focal line is positioned behind the retina, hyperopic defocus occurs, inducing posterior ocular growth.
  • Progressive Myopia Group: FS' focal line positioned behind retina → hyperopic defocus → posterior ocular growth → myopia progression
  • Non-progressive Myopia Group: Both FS' and FT' positioned in front of retina → myopic defocus → suppression of ocular growth

2. Changes in Retinal Curvature Radius and Peripheral Focal Position

  • Elliptical Shape: Most emmetropic/myopic eyes have an oblate elliptical shape where peripheral retinal curvature radius is smaller than central.
  • Myopia Progression and Retinal Changes: As myopia progresses, the posterior eye becomes steeper, and particularly as the nasal retina becomes steeper, FS' is positioned further behind the retina.

3. Optical Intervention for Myopia Control

  • Optical Design Principle: Orthokeratology, multifocal lenses, and power gradient contact lenses induce intentional myopic defocus in the periphery.
  • Mechanism of Action: Position peripheral FS' in front of the retina to act as an ocular growth suppression signal.

Key Research Findings

Importance of FS' Focal Line Position
Confirmed that when the FS' focal line is formed behind the retina in the peripheral area, hyperopic peripheral defocus occurs, inducing ocular growth and central myopia progression.
Retinal Curvature Radius and Myopia Progression
As retinal curvature radius decreases (steeper retina), hyperopic defocus increases in the periphery, inducing axial length elongation and causing myopia progression.
Efficacy of Optical Intervention
Myopia control lenses suppress ocular growth by positioning peripheral FS' in front of the retina, proving particularly effective when retinal curvature radius is steep.
Need for Complete Correction
Central refraction correction alone has limitations; complete correction considering the entire retinal refractive state is essential for myopia control.

Clinical Significance & Future Plans

This research provides more effective myopia control strategies by elucidating the fundamental mechanisms of myopia progression. The myopia control method through peripheral retinal focal position control is an innovative approach beyond simple central correction.

Future research will develop optical design algorithms customized for individual retinal curvature characteristics and establish a real-time myopia progression monitoring system. We also aim to develop a platform that provides personalized myopia control solutions through AI-based predictive models for early assessment of myopia progression risk.
