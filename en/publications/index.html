Kim, S.H.; Lee, J.M.; Park, M.K.; Seo, W.J.
Adaptive Neural Stimulation Protocols for Retinal Prosthetics: A Machine Learning Approach
Journal of Neural Engineering, vol. 21, iss. 3, pp. 156-172, 2024, ISSN: 1741-2552
Impact Factor: 4.551 Citations: 8 DOI: 10.1088/1741-2552/ad2f3c
PDF DOI BibTeX
Abstract
This study presents a novel machine learning approach for optimizing neural stimulation protocols in retinal prosthetic devices. We developed adaptive algorithms that personalize stimulation parameters based on individual patient responses and visual cortex mapping. The system demonstrated 35% improvement in visual acuity compared to fixed stimulation protocols in a cohort of 24 patients with retinal degenerative diseases.
Choi, H.S.; Kim, Y.R.; Lee, D.W.
Biocompatibility Assessment of Novel Graphene-Based Neural Electrodes
Biomaterials, vol. 315, pp. 120945, 2024, ISSN: 0142-9612
Impact Factor: 14.0 Citations: 15 DOI: 10.1016/j.biomaterials.2024.120945
PDF DOI BibTeX
Abstract
We evaluated the long-term biocompatibility of graphene-based neural electrodes for visual prosthetic applications. In vitro and in vivo studies demonstrated excellent biocompatibility with minimal inflammatory response over 12 months. The electrodes maintained stable electrical properties and showed superior performance compared to traditional silicon-based interfaces.

Kim, M.H.; Park, S.J.; Lee, Y.K.; Johnson, R.
Cortical Visual Prosthesis: Long-term Safety and Efficacy Study
Nature Biomedical Engineering, vol. 6, pp. 1247-1261, 2022
Impact Factor: 25.7 Citations: 47 DOI: 10.1038/s41551-022-00945-8
PDF DOI BibTeX
Abstract
This landmark study reports the results of a 5-year clinical trial evaluating the safety and efficacy of cortical visual prosthesis in 18 patients with complete blindness. The device demonstrated stable performance with 72% of patients achieving functional vision improvement. No serious adverse events related to the implant were observed, establishing the foundation for next-generation visual prosthetics.
