Phase 1: Visual Neuroscience

Deciphering the Neural Code

Before we can correct vision, we must understand how the brain processes it. Our research begins at the foundational level: analyzing how visual pathways interpret motion and contrast to distinguish between optical deficits and neurological processing issues.

Motion Contrast Sensitivity

Phase 2: Optical Engineering

Controlling the Eye through Light

We don't just correct vision; we actively manage eye growth. By analyzing the peripheral retina's focal plane, we develop optical interventions ("Total Correction") to stop myopia progression.

The Problem

Peripheral focal points triggering axial elongation (eye growth) due to standard lens designs.

The Observation

Steeper nasal retina pushing the focal line backwards, driving myopia progression.

The Solution

Induced myopic defocus effectively signaling the eye to stop growing using multi-segment optics.

Phase 3: Biometric Intelligence

The Eye as a Window to Health

By combining pupil dynamics, eye movement, and contrast sensitivity with AI, we can assess neurological and metabolic health non-invasively. Our platform detects signs of Diabetic Retinopathy, Glaucoma, and Cognitive Decline years before conventional symptoms.
