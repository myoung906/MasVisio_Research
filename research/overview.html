Motion Contrast Sensitivity in Visual Pathways

Investigating Hemispheric Asymmetry via Hemianopia Simulation

Objective

To verify if the left-hemisphere language dominance in right-handers extends to visual contrast sensitivity, and to analyze sensitivity to static and moving stimuli at 1 cpd low spatial frequency in the peripheral field.

Methodology

  • Subjects: 24 adults with normal vision (21 right-handed, 3 left-handed)
  • Equipment: Morphonome software, Hemianopia simulator
  • Conditions: Simulated Left Hemianopia (LH) vs Right Hemianopia (RH)
  • Stimuli: 1 cpd Gabor patches, 3 speed levels (0, 10, 30 cm/s)

Key Results

  • Significant Decrease Contrast sensitivity significantly decreased as stimulus speed increased (p<.001)< /span>
  • Pathway Comparison Trend of higher sensitivity in Right Pathway (RH) but not statistically significant (p=.075)

Mechanisms
